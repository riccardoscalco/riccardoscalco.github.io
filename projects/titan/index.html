Titan

The generative artworks show the recurrent intersections of simple geometric shapes. The initial circles and squares are positioned according to a regular grid, and the subsequent geometric shapes are centred at the intersections points, resulting in a balanced union between complexity and simplicity.

The project takes inspiration from the Huygens' principle, which states that every point on a wavefront is itself the source of spherical wavelets. Huygens was a Dutch physicist and astronomer, among his many contributions, he is also known for the discovery of Titan — the moon of Saturn.
